### H2: A Framework for Capturing Signals Without New Tools (400-500 words)
- **Answer-first**: The framework has three steps: Capture in context, Connect to accounts, Surface at decision time. PMs using organized feedback systems make product decisions 40% faster (Featurebase, 2026).
- **Step 1: Capture in context** - Use what you already have (Slack, email, meeting notes). The key: capture WHERE the signal happens, not in a separate tool.
- **Step 2: Connect to accounts** - Every signal needs two tags: WHO said it and WHAT it's about. Without this, signals are noise.
- **Step 3: Surface at decision time** - The signal's value is zero if it's not visible when the PM is making a prioritization call.

### H2: How to Start Tomorrow (200-300 words)
- **Answer-first**: You don't need to overhaul your workflow. Start with one meeting this week.
- Step 1: After your next customer call, spend 2 minutes noting: who was there, what they asked for, what's at risk
- Step 2: Tag it to the account and the feature/product it relates to
- Step 3: Before your next prioritization meeting, review the last 2 weeks of signals
- Build the habit before adding any tool

### FAQ Section (5 items)
1. **What are customer signals in B2B SaaS?** - Customer signals are any evidence from interactions that indicate what accounts need, value, or are at risk of. This includes feature requests, churn indicators, competitive mentions, adoption patterns, and strategic decisions. According to CX Today (2025), enterprise systems capture only 85-95% of expected customer events - meaning 5-15% of signals never enter any system at all.

2. **How many tools does the average product team use?** - The average enterprise manages 291 SaaS applications (Zylo, 2026), with at least 30% of licenses going unused. For product teams specifically, the stack typically includes a roadmap tool, feedback tool, analytics platform, CRM, project management tool, and communication tools - often 8-12 distinct applications that don't share context.

3. **What happens when customer signals get lost?** - Lost signals lead to uninformed prioritization. 49% of product managers identify "setting roadmap priorities without real market feedback" as their primary challenge (Mind the Product via Thematic, 2025). More critically, for every 1 customer who complains, 26 churn silently for the same reason (Gartner via Thematic) - meaning lost signals directly cause revenue loss.

4. **How do you reduce tool sprawl in product teams?** - Instead of adding another tool, create a signal capture layer within your existing workflow. Capture signals where they happen (Slack, email, call notes), connect them to accounts and features, and surface them at decision time. The goal isn't fewer tools - it's connected context across the tools you already use.

5. **How do you prioritize customer feedback for the product roadmap?** - Effective prioritization requires scoring signals across multiple dimensions: demand (how many accounts want this), risk (revenue at stake if you don't act), strategic alignment (does leadership care), and momentum (is signal volume increasing). PMs using organized feedback systems make product decisions 40% faster than those using manual processes (Featurebase, 2026).
